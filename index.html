Meet Steve. Steve the Llama



Before he was Steve the Llama, he was just a llama living with a multimillionaire in a trailer park.
From the admiration of one lucky owner, Steve has risen to earn the admiration of millions.
If you wish to learn about the struggles Steve faces, click any one of the portraits above.



Ever since Google named Steve "smart and easy to train" Steve worked hard to gain fame and fortune. Steve refuses to associate with anyone with less instagram followers than him.

Steve has had companies all over the world name themselves after him.

He's been mentioned by many rappers, who take great pride in him and remind the world Steve's presence is a privilege.

But fame didnt change him. Steve still keeps it real in his department.

Hi there!

Hi Im Anva, and I'm a great fan of Steve the Llama.
A little about me: I spent a large portion of my childhood living on a high hill over a tiny Mediterranean village before immigrating to the Big Apple. In my spare time you can find me practicing improv or locating jelly crepes.


When it comes to the front end, I will soon make ten months of being a self-taught coder. The idea of obtaining a position as a front end developer shifted from being a distant interest to a lifelong passion where I can translate my abstract thoughts onto clean code. I find it an ideal career with a load of perks and a never ending supply of fun things to learn!

The design of this website was inspired by a dark, futuristic atmosphere. I wanted the website to hold a sort of mystery as you scroll, yet still possessing a simple outlook. I'm a huge fan of parallax scrolling, css hover animations and of course, the color black which is why these three factors were implemented into the design. The theme of the content was humorous, purposely to contradict the darkness of the front end.


The website was purely built by HTML, CSS, JS&JQuery.
